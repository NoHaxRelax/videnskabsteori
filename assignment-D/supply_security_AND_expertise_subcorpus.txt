The Danish Energy Agency (Energistyrelsen) has assessed the socio-economic consequences of various
configurations of the Bornholm Energy Island project, which includes wind farms, a transformer station on
Bornholm, and grid connections to Denmark and Germany. The central estimates for the socio-economic impact
across all scenarios are negative, with potential losses ranging from 4 billion DKK to 17 billion DKK.
Additionally, the project is expected to require public subsidies amounting to 31.5 billion DKK over the first
approximately 20 years of operation. Of this amount, 17.6 billion DKK has already been politically approved as
of May 2023. The approved funding will be sourced from anticipated revenues from the 6 GW offshore wind tender
(8.7 billion DKK), the Green Fund (4.9 billion DKK), and future fiscal space (4.0 billion DKK). However, an
additional subsidy of approximately 14 billion DKK is needed because the wind farms associated with the energy
island are not expected to be profitable, and no specific financing has been identified for this amount. Given
the negative socio-economic outlook, the project should be reconsidered. The current socio-economic assessment
does not account for several effects that are difficult to quantify, such as energy supply security and
potential Power-to-X production. Therefore, it is recommended that a new socio-economic analysis be conducted,
incorporating as many relevant effects as possible. If it cannot be demonstrated that the project will
generate a socio-economic surplus, it should be abandoned.
